Rotating Turbulent Channel Flow
Coherent structures in streamwise rotating turbulent channel flow are studied by DNS and LES. Streamwise elongated large-scale vortices are observed through the conditional-sampling method. These structures are dominated by cyclones at unity rotation number. The cyclones prefer to develop in the lower and upper half channels, while the anti-cyclones, if exist, occur in the central region of the channel. This preference of the large-scale vortices in different regions contributes to the secondary flow. (Dai, Huang, Xu. 2019. PoF)
Drag Reduction
Drag reduction at the external surface of a cylinder in turbulent flows along the axial direction by circumferential wall motion is studied by direct numerical simulations. The circumferential wall oscillation can lead to drag reduction due to the formation of a Stokes layer, but it may also result in centrifugal instability, which can enhance turbulence and increase drag. The figure shows that the rotation-induced vortices are closely correlated with the high skin friction when the streamwise-travelling waves of circumferential wall velocity is applied to the flow. (Zhao, Huang, Xu. 2019. JFM)
Stability Analysis of Ellipsoids in Shear Flow
This paper has addressed the questions of the stability of rotations of spheroidal and tri-axial ellipsoids with various particle inertia and particle shapes. In addition, the stability difference between prefect spheroids and slightly tri-axial elongated/flat ellipsoids is also discussed. The results show that the stability of tri-axial ellipsoids is sensitive to shape at small St numbers, but the stabilities for the longestaxial and the shortest-axial rotations become neutrally stable with very large St numbers.(Cui, Zhao et al. 2019. PoF)
